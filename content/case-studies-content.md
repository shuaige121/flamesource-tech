### Case Study 1: Recruitment Consultancy — Automated Resume Screening

**Client type:** A Singapore-based recruitment consultancy placing candidates across multiple industries.

**Challenge:** The consultancy received a high volume of applications for every open role. Consultants spent the first part of each day reading through resumes manually, trying to separate strong candidates from poor fits. With the rise of AI-written resumes, many applications looked polished on the surface but lacked substance — making quick visual scanning unreliable. The real cost was not just time, but the risk of overlooking strong candidates buried in the pile.

**What we built:** We deployed an automated screening and ranking system that reads every incoming resume, scores candidates against role-specific criteria, and surfaces a ranked shortlist. The system handles formatting inconsistencies, parses experience and qualifications, and flags candidates whose backgrounds are genuinely relevant — regardless of how well the resume is formatted. Consultants now start their day with a shortlist rather than a stack.

**Delivery:** The system was deployed in approximately one month. Most of that time was spent in iterative alignment — understanding how the consultancy evaluates candidates, which criteria matter for different role types, and calibrating the ranking to match their judgment. Our response time during this process was fast; when the client flagged a misranked candidate, we adjusted and redeployed within hours, not days.

**Outcome:** Resume screening that previously consumed hours each morning now takes minutes. Consultants reported that they spend noticeably more of their day in conversations with candidates and hiring managers rather than reading documents. The client was very satisfied with both the speed of delivery and the ongoing responsiveness when adjustments were needed.

**Quote:**
> "We expected it to take much longer. The back-and-forth was quick — when we said a ranking didn't feel right, it was fixed the same day."
> — *Senior Recruitment Consultant*

### Case Study 2: Property Services Company — Rental Operations and Finance Automation

**Client type:** A Singapore property services company managing a portfolio of rental units across multiple locations.

**Challenge:** The company ran two labour-intensive workflows. On the operations side, managing tenancy records, lease renewals, move-in/move-out scheduling, and unit availability was a patchwork of spreadsheets and manual tracking. On the finance side, invoicing, payment reconciliation, and month-end reporting consumed days of manual data entry each cycle. The same staff often handled both workflows, meaning that operational urgencies during the month left finance tasks backlogged toward the end. Errors from manual entry created downstream reconciliation headaches.

**What we built:** We delivered two separate but connected automation solutions. The first structured the rental operations workflow — tenant records, unit status tracking, lease timelines, and task scheduling — into a single managed system. The second automated the finance workflow — invoice generation from tenancy data, payment matching, and reconciliation reporting. Because both solutions sat on top of the same client data, they reinforced each other: an updated lease automatically flowed into the correct invoice schedule.

**Delivery:** Both solutions were delivered within roughly one month. The iterative process was critical here. The client's rental operations had edge cases — partial-month leases, multi-unit tenants, variable billing schedules — that only surfaced through back-and-forth testing. We prioritised fast turnaround on each iteration. When the client identified a scenario we had not accounted for, the fix was typically delivered within a day.

**Outcome:** The monthly close process became significantly shorter. Manual data entry errors that previously caused reconciliation problems were eliminated because invoicing now flows directly from tenancy data. Staff who had been spending days on end-of-month finance tasks were freed to focus on tenant relationships and operational issues. The client noted that the biggest change was not just speed, but confidence — they trusted the numbers without needing to double-check everything manually.

**Quote:**
> "Before this, end-of-month was stressful every single time. Now the invoices match the tenancy records automatically. We actually trust what the system gives us."
> — *Operations Manager*

### Case Study 3: Multi-Outlet Chinese Restaurant — AR Smart Glasses and AI Staff Training

**Client type:** A multi-outlet Chinese restaurant group in Singapore employing Burmese service staff.

**Challenge:** Staff learned dish names and basic service flows quickly. The persistent problem was nuanced customer requests — "less oil", "crispier", "no chili", "don't overcook the vegetables". These modifiers are where dining preferences live, and they were regularly lost in translation. The issue was not willingness or intelligence; it was the gap between conversational fluency and the specific vocabulary of food preparation preferences. Wrong orders caused customer dissatisfaction, food waste, and frustration for staff who knew they had misunderstood but could not pinpoint what was said.

**What we built:** We deployed AR smart glasses that provide real-time subtitles during customer interactions. When a customer speaks, the staff member sees a translated transcription of the request — including the specific modifiers — displayed in their field of view. The interaction stays natural; there is no need to ask the customer to repeat themselves or to pull out a phone. Separately, we built an AI teacher app (accessible via web browser) that staff use during breaks to practise real customer phrases. The app drills them on the exact modifier vocabulary that causes problems on the floor, using spaced repetition and real recorded examples. Over time, staff internalise the language and rely on the glasses less.

**Delivery:** The full system was deployed in approximately one month. The iterative process was especially important here because the modifier vocabulary is domain-specific — "dry" means something different for noodles than for vegetables, and "spicy" has degrees that vary by dish. We worked closely with the restaurant's kitchen and floor managers to build an accurate phrase library. When staff or managers flagged a translation that felt unnatural or inaccurate, we updated the system the same day.

**Outcome:** Wrong orders caused by modifier misunderstandings were effectively eliminated. Staff reported feeling more confident during service, and managers noted that the dining experience improved because interactions flowed more naturally — customers no longer needed to repeat or simplify their requests. The AI teacher app saw consistent use during breaks, and several staff members progressed to the point where they understood most modifiers without checking the glasses. The client described this as one of the most tangible operational improvements they had made.

**Quote:**
> "The difference was immediate. Customers stopped having to repeat themselves, and the staff were visibly more confident. The training app was a bonus we didn't expect — some of the team are now picking up the phrases on their own."
> — *Restaurant Operations Manager*

### Case Study 4: Sales Coaching — Real-Time AI Assistance Across Multiple Industries

**Client type:** Multiple Singapore businesses in customer-facing sales roles — including a creative services studio, a study-abroad consultancy, and hair salons.

**Challenge:** In all of these businesses, the quality of a customer conversation directly determines whether a sale closes. New staff faced a steep learning curve: they knew the products or services, but lacked the instinct for when to recommend, how to handle objections, and how to close naturally. Experienced staff performed well but had no scalable way to transfer their knowledge. Traditional training — shadowing, role-play, manuals — helped, but there was a persistent gap between training and live performance. In hair salons, the challenge was compounded because staff must deliver a service and sell simultaneously.

**What we built:** We deployed a real-time AI coaching system built on speech-to-text. During a live customer conversation, the system transcribes the dialogue and provides the staff member with contextual coaching prompts — suggested responses, product recommendations, and objection-handling cues — in real time. The coaching is subtle and non-intrusive, designed so the customer never knows it is there. For new staff, we also built an AI practice partner: a conversational training tool that simulates realistic customer interactions, allowing staff to rehearse before going on the floor. The practice partner adapts to the staff member's weaknesses and focuses on the scenarios where they struggled most.

**Delivery:** Each deployment took approximately one month, with the majority of time spent on alignment — understanding each business's sales process, common customer objections, and what "good" looks like in their specific context. A creative services studio has very different sales dynamics from a hair salon, so the coaching logic was tailored for each. We responded quickly when clients said a coaching prompt felt off or a simulated scenario was unrealistic, typically adjusting within hours.

**Outcome:** New staff became productive on the floor significantly sooner than with previous training methods. Managers at all three types of businesses reported that new hires handled customer conversations with noticeably more confidence from their first week. Experienced staff appreciated the system as well — several noted that it occasionally surfaced recommendations they had not thought of. The practice partner was particularly valued by staff who were nervous about live selling, giving them a low-pressure environment to build skills. Clients across all deployments were very satisfied with the responsiveness of the delivery process.

**Quote:**
> "Our new consultant closed her first deal in her second week. She told me the practice sessions made her feel like she'd already had dozens of real conversations before her first one."
> — *Studio Director*

### Case Study 5: Emerging Music Artist — Arrangement and Instrumentation Acceleration

**Client type:** An emerging Singapore musician mentored by award-winning local music producers.

**Challenge:** The artist's creative process was fast — melodies and song structures came together quickly. But the arrangement and instrumentation phase was a bottleneck. Layering instruments, creating production-ready arrangements, and iterating on sonic direction took weeks for each track. For an emerging artist building momentum, that delay meant fewer releases, slower audience growth, and lost creative energy. The artist's mentors — established local producers with industry recognition — validated the musical direction, but the production pipeline could not keep up with the creative output.

**What we built:** We deployed tools that accelerated the arrangement and instrumentation workflow. The system takes the artist's core musical ideas — melody, chord progression, song structure — and generates arrangement drafts with instrumentation options. The artist reviews, selects, and refines from these drafts rather than building every layer from scratch. Creative decisions remain entirely with the artist and their mentors; the system handles the repetitive production work that previously consumed the most time. The output integrates with standard music production software, so the artist's existing workflow was not disrupted.

**Delivery:** Delivered in approximately one month. The iterative process was essential because musical taste is subjective and deeply personal. Early arrangement drafts did not match the artist's aesthetic — they were technically competent but stylistically off. Through fast back-and-forth, we refined the system's understanding of the artist's sonic preferences until the drafts consistently landed close to the intended direction. The artist noted that our response time made the process feel collaborative rather than transactional.

**Outcome:** Arrangement turnaround compressed from weeks to days per track. The artist was able to maintain creative momentum, moving from idea to production-ready arrangement without the long gaps that previously broke their flow. The quality of the final output — as assessed by the artist and their award-winning mentors — met professional production standards. The artist described the process as having a production partner who works at the speed of their ideas.

**Quote:**
> "I used to lose the feeling of a song by the time the arrangement was done. Now I can hear a full version while the idea is still fresh. And when something didn't sound right, the team fixed it the next day."
> — *Recording Artist*
